Introduction

This example uses the Bullet Physics engine to control the animation of the scene. In this example, we introduce a random midpoint displacement object. The program places a tower of sticks in the center of a random midpoint displacement terrain, key combinations will either drop or fire objects at the tower.

This example is the same as the first Bullet Tower example except that the material and lighting calculations are moved to the vertex shader. Since the lighting and material calculations are not as crucial in this application, most of the scene color is derived from the textures, they are done per-vertex instead of per fragment to increase the FPS speed.

User Options

  • Escape: Ends the program.
  • M: Toggles between fill mode and line mode to draw the triangles.
  • R: Resets the scene.
  • C: Resets the camera.
  • F10: Saves a screen shot of the graphics window to a png file.

If no modifier keys are pressed:

  • Left: Increases the yaw.
  • Right: Decreases the yaw.
  • Up: Increases the pitch.
  • Down: Decreases the pitch.

If the control or Z key is down:

  • Left: Increases the roll.
  • Right: Decreases the roll.
  • Up: Moves the camera forward.
  • Down: Moves the camera backward.

If the shift or S key is down:

  • Left: Moves the camera left.
  • Right: Moves the camera right.
  • Up: Moves the camera up.
  • Down: Moves the camera down.

These options are limited to 5 per second.

  • 1: Drops a cube.
  • 2: Drops a sphere.
  • 3: Drops a teapot.
  • 4: Drops a torus.
  • 5: Fires a box from the camera.
  • 6: Fires a sphere from the camera.
  • 7: Fires a sphere from a random point on a circle around the scene.
  • 8: Fires a box from a random point on a circle around the scene.
  • 9: Fires a teapot from a random point on a circle around the scene.
  • 0: Fires a donut (torus) from a random point on a circle around the scene.

If the F is down: These options are not time limited.

  • 1: Drops a cube.
  • 2: Drops a sphere.
  • 3: Drops a teapot.
  • 4: Drops a torus.
  • 5: Fires a box from the camera.
  • 6: Fires a sphere from the camera.
  • 7: Fires a sphere from a random point on a circle around the scene.
  • 8: Fires a box from a random point on a circle around the scene.
  • 9: Fires a teapot from a random point on a circle around the scene.
  • 0: Fires a donut (torus) from a random point on a circle around the scene.
Note
Note that the shader programs "VertexShaderLightingTexture.glsl", "PhongMultipleLightsAndTexture.glsl", "VertexShaderCubeMap.glsl", and "FragmentCubeMap.glsl" are expected to be in the same folder as the executable. Your graphics card must also be able to support OpenGL version 3.3 to run this program. The textures must be in the Textures folder that is located in the same folder as the executable.
